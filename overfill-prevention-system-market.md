<p><p>The Overfill Prevention System (OPS) market is categorized into two main types: automatic and manual systems. Automatic systems use sensors and control mechanisms to detect and prevent overfilling by automatically shutting off valves or alarms when fluid levels reach a designated limit. In contrast, manual systems rely on human intervention to monitor fluid levels and take action to prevent overfilling, often requiring operators to be present during filling operations. Each type serves important roles in various industries, enhancing safety and efficiency.</p></p>
