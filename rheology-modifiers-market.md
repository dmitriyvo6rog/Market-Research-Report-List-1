<p><p>The rheology modifiers market is categorized into two main types: organic and inorganic rheology modifiers. Organic rheology modifiers typically include natural and synthetic polymers, which enhance the flow and viscosity characteristics of various formulations, particularly in paints, coatings, and adhesives. In contrast, inorganic rheology modifiers, such as silica and clays, improve the stability and consistency of products by altering their viscoelastic properties. Both types play crucial roles in optimizing performance and processing in industries like automotive, construction, and personal care.</p></p>
