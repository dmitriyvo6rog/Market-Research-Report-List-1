<p><p>The antibiotics and antimycotics market is categorized into several types based on their chemical structure and mechanism of action. Penicillins and cephalosporins inhibit cell wall synthesis, while carbapenems offer broad-spectrum coverage against resistant bacteria. Macrolides target protein synthesis, and aminoglycosides disrupt bacterial functions. Quinolones (fluoroquinolones) interfere with DNA replication, whereas sulfonamides inhibit folic acid synthesis. Tetracyclines also block protein synthesis. The "Other" category includes diverse agents with varying mechanisms, addressing specific infections and resistance profiles.</p></p>
