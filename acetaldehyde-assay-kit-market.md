<p><p>The Acetaldehyde Assay Kit market is segmented into two primary detection types: colorimetric and spectrophotometric detection. Colorimetric detection employs a color change reaction to quantify acetaldehyde levels, allowing for straightforward visual interpretation. In contrast, spectrophotometric detection utilizes specific wavelengths of light to measure absorbance, providing precise quantitative results. Both methods cater to various laboratory and clinical applications, with colorimetric techniques often favored for simplicity and speed, while spectrophotometric methods are preferred for accuracy and sensitivity in complex samples.</p></p>
