<p><ul><li>Hydrothermal Synthesis</li><li>Solvothermal Synthesis</li><li>Electrochemical Deposition</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The synthetic iron sulfide market is categorized into three primary types based on production methods: hydrothermal synthesis, solvothermal synthesis, and electrochemical deposition. Hydrothermal synthesis involves reactions in high-temperature and pressure aqueous solutions, promoting controlled crystallization. Solvothermal synthesis uses organic solvents under similar conditions for enhanced material properties. In contrast, electrochemical deposition involves the electrochemical reduction of iron in sulfide solutions, allowing for precise thickness control and uniformity. Each method offers distinct advantages for the production of high-quality iron sulfide materials.</p></p>
