<p><p>Acute Intermittent Porphyria (AIP) management includes Gonadotropin-Releasing Hormone (GnRH) Analogues and Prophylactic Hematin Infusions. GnRH Analogues help reduce the frequency and severity of acute attacks by suppressing ovarian hormone production, which can trigger symptoms. Prophylactic Hematin Infusions provide a means to replenish heme levels in the body, countering the effects of porphyria and preventing attacks. Both treatment modalities aim to improve patients' quality of life and minimize the risk of acute episodes, contributing to a specialized market in AIP management.</p></p>
