<p><p>The urine analyzer market is primarily segmented into two types: microscopy and chemistry methods. The microscopy method involves examining urine samples under a microscope to identify cells, crystals, and microorganisms, providing valuable diagnostic insights. In contrast, the chemistry method employs various reagents and sensors to assess chemical properties such as pH, glucose, and protein levels in urine. Together, these methods enable comprehensive urine analysis, supporting accurate diagnosis and monitoring of various health conditions, contributing significantly to clinical laboratories and healthcare settings.</p></p>
