<p><p>The SBR lithium-ion battery binder market primarily consists of two types of polymerization processes: emulsion polymerization and suspension polymerization. Emulsion polymerization involves dispersing monomers in water, leading to the formation of polymer particles that enhance binder performance and stability. In contrast, suspension polymerization operates by suspending monomers in a liquid medium, creating larger, more robust polymer particles. Both methods significantly impact the electrochemical performance of lithium-ion batteries by improving adhesion, conductivity, and overall energy density.</p></p>
