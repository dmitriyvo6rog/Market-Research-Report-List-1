<p><p>Optical tweezers and magnetic tweezers are advanced tools used in mechanobiology to manipulate microscopic particles with precision. Optical tweezers utilize focused laser beams to trap and move biological samples, allowing for the study of molecular interactions and cell mechanics. In contrast, magnetic tweezers employ magnetic fields to exert forces on paramagnetic beads attached to biomolecules, enabling real-time observation of biochemical processes. Both technologies are crucial for exploring cellular responses to mechanical forces and understanding fundamental biological functions.</p></p>
